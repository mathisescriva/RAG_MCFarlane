## Summary of REG Section 4.30: Stability in Damaged Condition

### General Requirements
- **Intact Stability**: Ships must maintain sufficient intact stability to withstand flooding of any one main compartment within the floodable length (Ref: 4.30(1)).
- **Adjacent Compartment Flooding**: If two adjacent main compartments are separated by a stepped bulkhead, stability must be adequate to withstand flooding of both compartments (Ref: 4.30(2)).
- **Subdivision Factors**:
  - For a subdivision factor of **0.5 or less but more than 0.33**, stability must withstand flooding of any two adjacent compartments (Ref: 4.30(3)).
  - For a subdivision factor of **0.33 or less**, stability must withstand flooding of any three adjacent compartments (Ref: 4.30(4)).

### Technical Specifications
- **Calculation Requirements**: Stability calculations must consider ship design characteristics and be based on the worst anticipated service condition (Ref: 4.30(5)).
- **Water Flow Restrictions**: If decks or bulkheads restrict water flow, the Administration must ensure these restrictions are accounted for in stability calculations (Ref: 4.30(6)).
- **Righting Lever Curve**: The positive residual righting lever curve must have a minimum range of **15Â°** beyond the angle of equilibrium, which can be reduced to **10Â°** under certain conditions (Ref: 4.30(7)).
- **Area Under Righting Lever Curve**: Must be at least **0.015 metre-radians** from the angle of equilibrium to either the angle of progressive flooding or **22Â°** (one-compartment flooding) / **27Â°** (two or more compartments flooding) (Ref: 4.30(8)).
- **Residual Righting Lever**: Must be obtained within the range of positive stability, considering heeling moments from passenger crowding, launching survival craft, and wind pressure (Ref: 4.30(9)).
- **Heeling Moment Calculations**: Assumptions for heeling moments include:
  - **Crowding**: Four persons per square meter, each weighing **75 kg** (Ref: 4.30(10)(a)).
  - **Launching Craft**: Fully loaded lifeboats and liferafts assumed swung out ready for lowering (Ref: 4.30(10)(b)).
  - **Wind Pressure**: Assumed at **120 N/mÂ²** (Ref: 4.30(10)(c)).
- **Intermediate Flooding Conditions**: Maximum righting lever must be at least **0.05 metres** with a positive righting lever range of at least **7Â°** (Ref: 4.30(10)(d)).

### Damage Assumptions
- **Extent of Damage**: Longitudinal extent is **3 metres plus 3% of the ship's length (L)** or **11 metres**, whichever is less. For a subdivision factor of **0.33 or less**, the extent must include two consecutive main transverse watertight bulkheads (Ref: 4.30(12)(a)).
- **Transverse and Vertical Extent**: Transverse extent is **one-fifth of the breadth of the ship**, and vertical extent is from the baseline upwards without limit (Ref: 4.30(12)(b) and (c)).

### Unsymmetrical Flooding
- **Minimization**: Unsymmetrical flooding should be minimized (Ref: 4.30(13)(a)).
- **Heeling Angle**: Maximum angle of heel after flooding should not exceed **15Â°** (Ref: 4.30(13)(d)).
- **Equalization Time**: Time for equalization must not exceed **15 minutes** (Ref: 4.30(13)(e)).

- **Angle of Heel**: Should not exceed **7Â°** for one-compartment flooding and **12Â°** for simultaneous flooding of two or more compartments (Ref: 4.30(14)(b)).
